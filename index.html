Field notes

Six decisions worth reading about.

weight-engine.note

Grams inside, kilograms outside

Prices are defined per kg, but all internal math runs in integer grams — eliminating floating‑point drift in cart totals. Different weights of one product become separate cart lines via unique keys, and the per‑kg price is snapshotted at add‑to‑cart time so later edits never corrupt an open cart.

carousel.note

Drag‑to‑scroll that works over links

Product cards are anchors wrapping images — native drag ghosting and navigation fight pointer capture. The fix: document‑level mouse events with preventDefault, draggable=false, click suppression after drag, and snap disabled mid‑drag. Buttery in RTL, on every browser tested.

caching.note

Surviving aggressive edge caching

The production host minifies and re‑serves every asset under content‑hash URLs. The theme leans into it: new files earn new hashes automatically, critical overrides ship in dedicated late‑loading files, and @supports fallbacks keep old browsers from ever seeing broken glass.

seo.note

Meta for products that don’t exist yet

Descriptions and focus keyphrases generate at render time via filter hooks — not stored per product. Every future product inherits rich, localized meta the moment it’s published. Zero maintenance, forever.

recovery.note

Un‑corrupting a catalog with zero downtime

A bad CSV import spawned 575 duplicates overnight. Recovery ran date‑scoped batch operations against the live store — trashing only the duplicates, verifying originals kept their images and categories, and cross‑checking final counts. No downtime, no lost products.

barcode.note

A barcode scanner on the owner’s phone

Enrichment needed barcodes as a join key against a 7,334‑row supplier dataset. Instead of hardware, the project shipped a self‑contained HTML5 scanner — phone camera, live decode, local storage, CSV export — served over HTTPS so it works anywhere in the store.
